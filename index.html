BRIEF BIO: I'm currently an undergraduate student at KAIST. During my time at a science high school, I conducted various research related to neurobiology and bioengineering. Most recently, I studied how to predict genotypes and improve data bias using artificial neural network algorithms.

I have a broad interest in statistical inference models, information theory, and artificial intelligence models for research. I am also interested in lab-on-a-chip research using microfluidics.

Research Overview

I am currently interested in the following three key research areas:

Area 1: Optics & Photonics

Optics and Photonics is the interdisciplinary field concerned with the fundamental science of light, its generation, detection, and manipulation, and the technological application of these principles in systems ranging from telecommunications to quantum computing.

Area 2: Signal Processing

Signal processing focuses on algorithms and hardware for analyzing, modifying and synthesizing signals and data, across a wide variety of application domains.

Area 3: Lab-on-a-Chip

Lab-on-a-Chip is a multidisciplinary field focusing on the miniaturization and integration of multiple laboratory functions—such as sample preparation, chemical synthesis, and analysis—onto a single, microfabricated device typically the size of a few square centimeters.
